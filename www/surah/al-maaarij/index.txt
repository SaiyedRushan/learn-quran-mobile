19:T73c,Al-Ma'aarij opens with a taunt made into a prayer: a mocker demands that the promised punishment fall — right now, if it is real. The surah answers not by hurrying the punishment but by naming its Source: it is from Allah, <em>Owner of the ascending ways</em> (<em>dhi l-ma'ārij</em>), the One to whom the angels and the Spirit ascend on a Day whose measure is fifty thousand years. To the mockers that Day looks impossibly distant; to Allah it is near. So the Prophet ﷺ is told: <em>be patient with a beautiful patience</em>. Then the terror is unveiled — a sky like molten metal, mountains like tufts of wool, and a moment so overwhelming that no close friend even asks after his friend, and the guilty man would ransom himself from the punishment with his own children, his wife, his brother, his whole clan, everyone on earth — anything to escape. But no: it is <em>Lazā</em>, the Flame that strips away the very skin.

From that scene the surah turns to diagnose the human heart itself: man was created <em>halū'</em> — anxious and grasping — panicking when evil touches him and hoarding when good does.
