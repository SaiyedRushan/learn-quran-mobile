19:T5e3,Surah Nooh is a single, sustained portrait of one prophet's struggle. Allah sends Nūh to warn his people before a painful punishment overtakes them, and his message is stripped to its essentials: worship Allah alone, fear Him, and obey the messenger He sent. Turning to his Lord, Nūh lays out the story of his own preaching — he called them <em>night and day</em>, in public and in private, openly and in whispered confidence — and every method met the same wall. They stuffed their fingers in their ears, pulled their garments over their heads, and grew only more arrogant. At the heart of his call is one of the Qur'an's most practical teachings: seek your Lord's forgiveness, for He is the Perpetual Forgiver — and He will answer with rain from the sky, wealth and children, gardens and flowing rivers.
